<p><p>Knitted fabrics are divided into two main types: weft knitted fabrics and warp knitted fabrics. Weft knitted fabrics are created by interlocking loops of yarn in a horizontal direction, resulting in a stretchy and flexible fabric. On the other hand, warp knitted fabrics are made by interlocking loops of yarn in a vertical direction, creating a more stable and durable fabric. Both types of knitted fabrics have unique characteristics and are used in a variety of applications in the textile industry.</p></p>
