<p><p>Seaweed source hydrocolloids are derived from various components of seaweed, including cellulose and derivatives, hemicellulose, pectin, exudate gums, and other sources. Cellulose and derivatives are used for their thickening and stabilizing properties. Hemicellulose is another type of hydrocolloid that is commonly used in the food industry. Pectin is a gelling agent often found in jams and jellies. Exudate gums are natural gums that are extracted from plants. Other sources of seaweed hydrocolloids include agar, carrageenan, and alginate.</p></p>
