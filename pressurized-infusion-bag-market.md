<p><p>A pressurized infusion bag is a medical device used to deliver medication or fluids to a patient's bloodstream through intravenous (IV) administration. These bags are typically made of polyvinyl chloride (PVC) or polyethylene materials, which are flexible and can withstand pressure while maintaining sterility. Pressurized infusion bags have a designated pressure release valve that allows excess air to escape while the bag is being pressurized.</p><p>The pressurized infusion bag market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 4.6% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this market growth. Firstly, there has been an increasing prevalence of chronic diseases and a rise in the number of hospital admissions, creating a higher demand for IV administration. Additionally, technological advancements in pressurized infusion bag systems, such as the integration of smart features, improved safety mechanisms, and compatibility with various administration sets and pumps, have further stimulated market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the growing geriatric population and increasing healthcare expenditure in developing countries are also significant factors driving the market. These bags serve different medical needs and offer diverse pressure control methods.</p></p>
