<p><p>Augmented reality (AR) refers to the technology that overlays digital information onto the user's real-world environment. It enhances the user's perception and interaction with the physical world. On the other hand, virtual reality (VR) creates a completely immersive digital environment where users can interact and perceive as if it were real.</p><p>In the healthcare sector, AR and VR have emerged as powerful tools with numerous applications. AR is being used to provide real-time information to doctors during surgical procedures, allowing them to access patient data without taking their eyes off the operation. It can also assist in medical training by providing interactive and realistic simulations. VR, on the other hand, is used in pain management, phobia treatment, physical therapy, and rehabilitation programs. It can create virtual environments that help patients distract themselves from pain or engage in therapeutic activities.</p><p>The market for AR and VR in healthcare is projected to grow at a CAGR of 13.6% during the forecast period. The increasing adoption of these technologies in medical training and education is driving market growth.
