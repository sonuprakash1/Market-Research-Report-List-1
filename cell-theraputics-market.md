<p><p>The cell therapeutics market is comprised of two main types: stem cells and immunocytes. Stem cells are undifferentiated cells that have the potential to develop into different types of cells in the body, making them valuable for regenerative medicine and tissue repair. Immunocytes, on the other hand, are a type of white blood cells that are crucial for immune system function. Immunocyte-based therapies aim to modulate or enhance the body's immune response to fight against diseases such as cancer. Both stem cells and immunocytes represent promising avenues for medical advancements and research.</p></p>

<p><p>The cell therapy market includes three key applications: allogeneic cell therapy, autologous cell therapy, and xenogeneic cell therapy.
