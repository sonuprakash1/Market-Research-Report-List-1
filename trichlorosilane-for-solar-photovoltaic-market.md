<p><p>Trichlorosilane is a key material in the production of silicon used in solar photovoltaic panels. It is produced through two main processes: Direct Chlorination (DC) and Hydrochlorination (HC). The DC process involves reacting silicon with chlorine gas at high temperatures, while the HC process involves reacting silicon tetrachloride with hydrogen gas. Both processes result in the production of trichlorosilane, which is then used to produce high-purity silicon for solar panels. These processes are crucial in the growing solar photovoltaic market.</p></p>
