<p><p>Fibre cement cladding is a type of building material made from a mixture of cellulose fibers, cement, and sand. <p><p>Fibre cement cladding can be classified into three main types based on density: low density, medium density, and high density. Low density fibre cement cladding is lightweight and easy to install, making it a popular choice for residential buildings. Medium density types offer a balance between weight and durability, suitable for both residential and commercial applications. High density fibre cement cladding is the most durable and is typically used in high-rise buildings and industrial structures due to its strength and resistance to impact and fire.</p></p>
