<p><p>Osteosynthesis devices are medical instruments and implants used in orthopedic surgeries to stabilize and restore fractured bones. They aid in the healing process by providing support and alignment to the affected bone, allowing for better recovery and mobility. These devices are made from biocompatible materials, such as titanium or stainless steel, and are available in various forms like plates, screws, rods, pins, and wires.</p><p>The global osteosynthesis devices market is expected to experience substantial growth during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to factors such as an increasing geriatric population, a rise in the number of sports-related injuries, and advancements in surgical techniques. The growing prevalence of orthopedic diseases, such as osteoporosis and arthritis, further contributes to the demand for osteosynthesis devices.</p><p>Advancements in technology have led to the development of new and innovative osteosynthesis devices. For instance, the introduction of bioabsorbable implants has gained popularity as they eliminate the need for a second surgery to remove the device. Additionally, the increasing preference for minimally invasive surgeries has led to the adoption of osteosynthesis devices that facilitate faster recovery, reduced scarring, and fewer complications.</p><p>Geographically, North America holds a significant share in the global market due to the presence of well-established healthcare infrastructure, high healthcare expenditure, and favorable reimbursement policies.
