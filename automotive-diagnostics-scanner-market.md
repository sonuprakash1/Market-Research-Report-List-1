<p><p>Automotive diagnostic scanners come in two main types: corded and cordless (Bluetooth). Corded scanners connect directly to the vehicle's port using a physical cable, providing a stable and secure connection for data transmission. On the other hand, cordless scanners use Bluetooth technology to connect wirelessly to the vehicle, offering more flexibility and convenience for technicians. Both types of scanners are widely used in the automotive industry for diagnosing and troubleshooting issues with vehicles, providing essential data for repairs and maintenance.</p></p>
