<p><p>Microtome blades are cutting tools used in histology laboratories to prepare thin slices of tissue samples for microscopic examination. The market for microtome blades can be categorized into two types: disposable blades and permanent blades. Disposable blades are made of high-quality stainless steel and are used for one-time use only, ensuring sharp and precise cuts. Permanent blades, on the other hand, are made of durable materials like tungsten carbide and can be resharpened multiple times, making them more cost-effective in the long run. Both types offer different advantages depending on the lab's requirements and budget.</p></p>
