<p><p>Antidiabetic sulphonylureas are classified into three generations based on their chemical structure and properties. First-generation sulphonylureas include chlorpropamide and tolbutamide, which are less potent and have a higher risk of adverse effects. The second generation, such as glibenclamide and glipizide, offer improved efficacy and fewer side effects. The third generation sulphonylureas, like glimepiride and gliclazide, are even more advanced with increased selectivity and potency, resulting in better glycemic control and reduced hypoglycemia risk. This progressive development of sulphonylureas reflects a continuous effort to enhance their effectiveness and tolerability in managing diabetes.</p></p>
