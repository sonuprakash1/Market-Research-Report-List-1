<p><p>Breathable bags come in two main types: Micro Perforation and Macro Perforation. Micro Perforation involves small holes punched into the bag, allowing for minimal airflow and moisture release. This type is often used for packaging delicate items like fruits and vegetables. On the other hand, Macro Perforation involves larger holes that allow for greater airflow and moisture release. This type is often used for items that require more ventilation, such as baked goods or popcorn. Both types cater to different market needs based on the level of breathability required for the products being packed.</p></p>
