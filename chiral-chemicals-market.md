<p><p>The chiral chemicals market can be broadly categorized into three types of separation methods. Firstly, traditional separation methods involve the use of conventional techniques like distillation or crystallization to separate chiral compounds. Secondly, asymmetric preparation methods use chiral catalysts or reagents to selectively synthesize one enantiomer over the other. Lastly, biological separation methods involve the use of enzymes or microorganisms to selectively separate chiral compounds. Each of these methods has its own advantages and applications in industries such as pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and flavors and fragrances.</p></p>
