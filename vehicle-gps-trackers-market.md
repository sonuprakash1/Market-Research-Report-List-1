<p><p>Vehicle GPS trackers are available in two main types: wired and wireless. Wired vehicle GPS trackers are devices that are physically connected to the vehicle's power source for continuous tracking. They are typically more reliable and have a longer lifespan but require professional installation. On the other hand, wireless vehicle GPS trackers use cellular networks to transmit location data, offering flexibility and easy installation. Both types offer real-time tracking, geofencing, and other features to help monitor and manage vehicle fleets efficiently.</p></p>
