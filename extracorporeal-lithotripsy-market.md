<p><p>Extracorporeal lithotripsy is a medical procedure used to treat kidney stones. The market for this procedure is categorized into three types: electrohydraulic technique, electromagnetic technique, and piezoelectric technique. The electrohydraulic technique involves using shock waves generated externally to break down the stones. The electromagnetic technique uses a magnetic field to induce shock waves, while the piezoelectric technique utilizes crystals that produce shock waves when an electric current is applied. These different techniques offer alternatives for treating kidney stones, providing physicians and patients with various options for effective stone fragmentation.</p></p>
