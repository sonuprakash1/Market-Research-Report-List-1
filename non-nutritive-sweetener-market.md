<p><p>Non-nutritive sweeteners are classified into two main types: artificial sweeteners and natural sweeteners. Artificial sweeteners are chemically synthesized sugar substitutes, providing intense sweetness without the calories of sugar. Examples include aspartame, Sucralose, and saccharin. Natural sweeteners, on the other hand, are derived from plants or fruits and are perceived as more wholesome alternatives to artificial sweeteners. Examples include stevia, monk fruit, and erythritol. Both types cater to the growing demand for lower-calorie sweetening options in the food and beverage industry.</p></p>
