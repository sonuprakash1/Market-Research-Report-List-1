<p><p>Parkinson's Disease Drugs market can be categorized into three main types based on administration methods: oral, transdermal, subcutaneous, and intestinal infusion. Oral medications are taken by mouth and widely used as a first-line treatment. Transdermal drugs are delivered through the skin using patches, providing a continuous release of medication. Subcutaneous injections deliver drugs under the skin, offering a more direct route to the bloodstream. Intestinal infusion involves delivering medications directly to the small intestine, bypassing the stomach for improved absorption.</p></p>
