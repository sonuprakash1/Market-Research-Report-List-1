<p><p>Computer Aided Engineering (CAE) refers to the application of computer software to aid in engineering analysis and simulation. Finite Element Analysis (FEA) is a CAE method that uses a numerical technique to analyze the structural behavior of materials, components, and systems. Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) is another CAE method that uses numerical algorithms to analyze and simulate fluid flow and heat transfer. FEA focuses on the mechanical behavior of objects, while CFD is concerned with fluid flow and heat transfer analysis.</p></p>
