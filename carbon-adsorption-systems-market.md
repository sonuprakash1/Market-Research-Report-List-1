<p><ul><li>Deep Bed Adsorber</li><li>Parallel Bed Adsorber</li><li>Carbon Tray Adsorber</li><li>Carbon Filter Adsorber</li><li>Multiple Adsorber Vessel System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Carbon adsorption systems are used to remove contaminants from air or water by trapping them on activated carbon. Different types of carbon adsorption systems include deep bed adsorber, parallel bed adsorber, carbon tray adsorber, carbon filter adsorber, and multiple adsorber vessel system. Deep bed adsorbers use a thick layer of carbon for efficient filtration, while parallel bed adsorbers have multiple beds for continuous operation. Carbon tray adsorbers use trays filled with carbon, and carbon filter adsorbers use carbon in a filter format. Multiple adsorber vessel systems utilize multiple vessels for higher capacity and flexibility in adsorption processes.</p></p>
