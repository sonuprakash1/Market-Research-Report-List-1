<p><p>Handheld Doppler ultrasound systems are used for various medical applications, including detecting and measuring blood flow. Directional Doppler systems are able to measure blood flow in a specific direction, making them useful for evaluating conditions such as peripheral arterial disease. Non-directional Doppler systems, on the other hand, provide a more general assessment of blood flow, making them suitable for tasks like locating veins for IV placement. Both types of Doppler systems play important roles in healthcare settings for diagnosing and monitoring patients.</p></p>
